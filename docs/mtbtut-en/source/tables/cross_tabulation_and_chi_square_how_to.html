Description of how to perform Cross Tabulation and Chi-Square using Minitab  

Scenario

A researcher wants to assess if there is an association between gender and their candidate preference (Democrats, Republicans, and Others) in a presidential election. Gender and candidate preference is recorded for 400 randomly selected individuals.

Choose the appropriate analysis

The researcher wants to analyze the association between two categorical variables, so she uses Cross Tabulation and Chi-Square.
  1. Open the worksheet POLITICS.MTW.
  2. Choose Stat > Tables > Cross Tabulation and Chi-Square.

Enter your data

To create a table of your categorical data, specify the variable that corresponds to the table's rows and the variable that corresponds to the table's columns.
  1. In Rows, enter Gender.
  2. In Columns, enter Preference.

Perform Chi-Square analysis

Click Chi-Square to perform this analysis on the tabulated data. The analysis will indicate whether an association between the row and column variables exists.
  1. Click Chi-Square.
  2. Check Chi-Square test, Expected cell counts, Raw residuals, Standardized residuals, Adjusted residuals, and Each cell's contribution to chi-square.
  3. Click OK in each dialog box.

Specify options

This analysis requires no further modifications, but other options are available. Click Other Stats to request additional statistics and tests of association and independence. Click Options to control how Minitab handles marginal statistics and missing values.
  • (no action required)
