Description of how to perform Binary Logistic Regression using Minitab  

Scenario

A cereal company is investigating the effectiveness of a TV advertisement for a new product called Cocoa Crunch. After showing the advertisement in a particular community for one week, they randomly sampled seventy-one adults exiting a local supermarket.

They were asked:
  • Whether or not they bought Cocoa Crunch (Bought)
  • Their household income (in thousands of dollars) (Income)
  • Whether or not they had children (Children)
  • Whether or not they viewed the advertisement (ViewAd)

Choose the appropriate analysis

The company wants to model a binary outcome with a variety of predictors, so they use Binary Logistic Regression.
  1. Open the worksheet CEREALAD.MTW.
  2. Choose Stat > Regression > Binary Logistic Regression > Fit Binary Logistic Model.

Enter your response variable

The company has a column of binary data for the response variable.
  1. Choose Response in response/frequency format.
  2. In Response, enter Bought.

Enter the terms in your model

Enter the predictor variables in the space for each type.
  1. In Continuous predictors, enter Income.
  2. In Categorical predictors, enter Children ViewAd.
  3. Click OK.

Specify options

This regression analysis requires no further modifications, but other options are available. To include interactions or higher order terms, click Model. To change the link function or make other analysis modifications, click Options. To change the increment of continuous predictors for odds ratios, control how Minitab codes categorical predictors or to standardize continuous predictors, click Coding. If you want to use a stepwise procedure to choose your model, click Stepwise. To create a variety of diagnostic plots, click Graphs. To control the display of results, click Results. To store various statistics, click Storage.
  • (no action required)
