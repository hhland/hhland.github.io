Description of how to perform General Regression using Minitab  

Scenario

A pharmaceutical company wants to model the relationship between the salaries of research scientists and their gender, years of experience, and an index of publication quality. The company wants to test the interactions between all predictors.

Choose the appropriate analysis

The company wants to create a regression model with both continuous and categorical predictors and interactions between predictors, so they use General Regression.
  1. Open the worksheet RESEARCHERSALARY.MTW.
  2. Choose Stat > Regression > Regression > Fit Regression Model.

Enter your response variable

Enter a single column of measurements for the response variable.
  1. In Responses, enter Salary.

Enter the terms in your model

Enter a column of data for each predictor variable.

Fit Regression Model can include both categorical and continuous predictors in the model.
  1. In Continuous predictors, enter Publication Years.
  2. In Categorical predictors, enter Gender.

Further specify your model

To specify interaction terms and polynomial terms, click Model.

These terms do not necessarily correspond to a single column of data. In this example, the company includes the two-way interactions.
  1. Click Model.
  2. Under Predictors, press the Ctrl key and select Publication Years Gender.
  3. To the right of Interactions through order, choose 3, and click Add.
  4. Click OK.

Specify options

To create residual plots, click Graphs. To change the confidence level, transform nonnormal data with the Box-Cox transformation, and other aspects of the analysis, click Options. To control how Minitab codes categorical predictors and to standardize continuous predictors, click Coding. If you want to use a stepwise procedure to choose your model, click Stepwise. To control the display of analysis results, click Results. To store regression statistics, click Storage.
  1. Click Graphs.
  2. Under Residual plots, choose Four in one.
  3. Under Residuals versus the variables, enter Years.
  4. Click OK in each dialog box.
