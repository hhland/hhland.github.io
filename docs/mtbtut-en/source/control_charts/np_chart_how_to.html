Description of how to creating an NP chart using Minitab  

Scenario

A manufacturer of light bulbs wants to assess whether or not its process is in control. Samples of light bulbs were taken every hour for three shifts (24 samples of 500 light bulbs) and tested to see whether or not they light. Defectives are light bulbs that do not light.

Choose the appropriate analysis

Because defectives are being monitored, you can use either a P or NP chart. Because subgroup size (number of light bulbs tested) is constant, you can choose the one that you prefer.
  1. Open the worksheet BULBDEFECT.MTW.
  2. Choose Stat > Control Charts > Attributes Charts > NP.

Enter your data

You have one column that contains the defectives data. Specify the subgroup size with an integer. If the subgroup size varies, you can still produce an NP chartÂ—just enter the column that contains the subgroup sizes instead.
  1. In Variables, enter Defectives.
  2. In Subgroup sizes, enter 500.

Specify options

This chart offers many helpful options. You can add labels, split the chart into stages, subset the data used for your chart, and so on. In this case, you are concerned with not missing any possible special causes of variation.
  1. Click NP-Chart options, and then choose Tests.
  2. Choose Perform all tests for special causes, and then click OK in each dialog box.
