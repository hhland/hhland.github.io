Data requirements for an Xbar-S chart.  

What kind of data is required?

You must consider only one numeric measure per control chart. For example:
  • Tensile strength of copper wire
  • Fill weight of cereal boxes
  • Time to change a car's oil
  • Your weight or blood pressure
Consider the size, frequency, and number of subgroups to be charted. Your sample size (subgroup size) should be 9 or greater. (If your sample size is 8 or smaller, use an Xbar-R Chart.) To be representative of the process, all of the samples must be within a subgroup at the same time. For example:
  • Measure the tensile strength for 12 pieces of wire every 4 hours
  • Measure the fill weight for 15 boxes of cereal each hour
  • Select 10 work orders and determine how long workers needed to change the oil
  • Take 10 blood pressure measurements each day at the same time

Choose subgroups so that differences among measurements within the same subgroup are small and so that you can detect differences between subgroups. For initial process studies, subgroups of 4 or 5 are measured every hour or so are typical (and would require an Xbar-R chart). As the process demonstrates stability (or as improvements are made), you can decrease the subgroup size and frequency.

Collect subgroups for a duration that is long enough to assure that major sources of variation have the chance to occur. Usually 25 or more subgroups is enough.

Generally, industry prefers small, frequent samples to signal a process shift before too much defective product is made.

What should my worksheet look like?

Samples in one column

Data are contained in one column, with subgroup identifiers in a second column.

Note If you have unequal subgroup sizes, you must use this layout.
C1 C2
Strength Subgroup

2 21.3 1
3 20.9 1
4 21.7 2
5 22.2 2
6 22.1 2

Samples in different rows

Data for each subgroup are in separate rows.

Samples in different rows

You can also use the date and time the sample was taken as a subgroup identifier. For example, subgroup 1 was taken 9/1 06:00 AM and subgroup 2 was taken 9/1 10:00 AM.
