Description of how to perform a Gage Linearity and Bias Study using Minitab  

Scenario

An automotive supplier wants to evaluate the accuracy of their measuring device to measure length of an engine part. Five parts were chosen that represented the expected range of measurements. Each part was measured by a layout inspector to determine its master measurement, then one operator randomly measured each part 12 times. In an earlier Gage R&R study, inspectors determined the total study variation was 14.1941. They decide to use this value as the historical process variation.

Choose the appropriate analysis

Because the manager wants to know if the measuring device is accurate, it is appropriate to use a Gage Linearity and Bias Study.
  1. Open the worksheet MEASUREMENTQUALITY.MTW.
  2. Choose Stat > Quality Tools > Gage Study > Gage Linearity and Bias Study.

Enter your data

You must include Part numbers, reference values, and measurement data.
  1. In Part numbers, enter Part.
  2. In Reference values, enter Reference.
  3. In Measurement data, enter Response.

Enter an historical process variation

You can specify the process variation by using your process knowledge or the total study variation value from a previously conducted Gage R&R Study (Crossed).
  1. In Process variation, enter 14.1941.
  2. Click OK.

Specify options

This gage study requires no further modifications, but other options are available. You can specify information about the gage, change the method for estimating repeatability standard deviation, or add a title for the graph.
  • (no action required)
