Description of how to perform a Gage R&R Study (Crossed) using Minitab  

Scenario

An automotive supplier wants to know if the currently used measurement system is precisely measuring the thickness of an engine part.

Ten parts that represent the expected range of the process variation were selected. Three operators measured the thickness of each of the ten parts, two times per part, in a random order.

Choose the appropriate analysis

Because the supplier wants to know if he can trust his data and multiple operators measure the same part, it is appropriate to use a Gage R&R Study (Crossed).
  1. Open the worksheet THICKNESS.MTW.
  2. Choose Stat > Quality Tools > Gage Study > Gage R&R Study (Crossed).

Enter your data

You must include part numbers and measurement data, but you do not have to include operators. If you don't include operators in your analysis, Minitab does not include an estimate of reproducibility variation in your results.
  1. In Part numbers, enter Part.
  2. In Operators, enter Operator.
  3. In Measurement data, enter Response.

Choose method of analysis

The calculations used in the Xbar and R method are simpler; however, the ANOVA method is more accurate. The ANOVA method also breaks down reproducibility into its operator, and operator by part, components.
  1. Under Method of Analysis, choose ANOVA.

Specify options

Many options are available with this gage R&R study. In this example, the historical standard deviation and process tolerance are known, so they should be entered in Options.
  1. Click Options.
  2. Under Process tolerance, choose Upper spec - Lower spec and enter 1.
  3. In Historical standard deviation, enter 0.174757.
  4. Click OK in each dialog box.
