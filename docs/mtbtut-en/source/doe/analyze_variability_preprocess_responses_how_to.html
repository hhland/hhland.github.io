Description of how to using Analyze Variability and Preprocess Responses in Minitab  

Scenario

A building material manufacturer is developing a new insulation product for construction. The manufacturer studied four factors that were believed to impact the strength of the insulation. The four factors are:
  • Material type (Material)
  • Injection pressure (InjPress)
  • Injection temperature (InjTemp)
  • Cooling temperature (CoolTemp)

After examining the mean strength, they decided to conduct another experiment to examine the effects of the four factors on the variability in insulation strength.

Calculate standard deviations

Before you can analyze variability in your response data, you must either calculate and store the standard deviations and counts of your repeat or replicate responses, or you must specify standard deviations that you have already stored in the worksheet. In this example, this information has already been calculated and recorded.
  1. Open InsulationStrength.MTW.
  2. Choose Stat > DOE > Factorial > Preprocess Responses for Analyze Variability.
  3. Under Standard deviations to use for analysis, choose Standard deviations already in worksheet.
  4. Under Use Std Devs in, enter Std.
  5. Under Use Counts, enter N.
  6. Click OK.

Analyze the design using least squares regression

After you pre-process the response data, you are ready to determine which effects to include in the model. Use least squares regression for this part of the analysis.
  1. Choose Stat > DOE > Factorial > Analyze Variability.
  2. In Response (standard deviations), enter Std.
  3. Click Terms.
  4. In Include terms in the model up through order choose 2.
  5. Click Options.
  6. Under Estimation method, choose Least squares regression.
  7. Click OK in each dialog box.

Add graphs to your output

Create effects plots to identify influential factors. When you have a final model, create residual plots to check assumptions of normality, constant variance, and randomness. Residual plots also help you to see if another variable affects the response or if outliers are present.
  1. Click Graphs.
  2. Under Effects Plots, check Pareto, Normal, and Half Normal.
  3. Under Residual Plots, check Three in one.
  4. Click OK in each dialog box.

Analyze the design using maximum likelihood estimation

After you use least squares regression to choose the model, you are ready to estimate the coefficients. Use maximum likelihood analysis for this part of the analysis.
  1. Choose Stat > DOE > Factorial > Analyze Variability.
  2. In Response (standard deviations), enter Std.
  3. Click Options.
  4. Under Estimation method, choose Maximum likelihood.
