Description of how to a using Minitab  

Scenario

A chemical manufacturer is trying to maximize the yield of a catalytic reaction. The researchers chose a central composite design to study the effects of time and temperature on the yield of the reaction. Because the data was collected on two different days, the design contains two blocks.

Enter response data in worksheet

After you create the design, conduct the experiment, collect the data, and enter the data in the Minitab worksheet. A worksheet is already prepared for you to analyze.
  1. Open CatalyticReaction.MTW.

Specify your model

You can add and remove individual terms from your model or specify the order of the model. For this example, you want to include main effects, the interaction between Time and Temp, and the quadratic terms for Time and Temp.
  1. Choose Stat > DOE > Response Surface > Analyze Response Surface Design.
  2. In Responses, enter Yield.
  3. Click Terms.
  4. From Include terms in the model up through order, choose Full quadratic.
  5. Click OK.

Add graphs to your output

When you have a final model, create residual plots to check assumptions of normality, constant variance, and randomness. Residuals plots also help you see if another variable affects the response or if outliers are present.
  1. Click Graphs.
  2. Under Residuals Plots, choose Four in one.
  3. Check Residuals versus variables and enter Instrument.
  4. Click OK in each dialog box.

Specify options

This analysis requires no further modifications, but other options are available. To change the confidence level, transform nonnormal data with the Box-Cox transformation, and other aspects of the analysis, click Options. If you want to use a stepwise procedure to choose your model, click Stepwise. To modify the display of analysis results, click Results. To store response surface statistics, click Storage.
  • (no action required)

Interpreting the output

Now what? For guidance on interpreting the results of this analysis, see StatGuide. To see a related tutorial that creates this design, see Create Response Surface Designs. For a tutorial on graphs associated with this type of design, see Graphs for Response Surface DOE.
