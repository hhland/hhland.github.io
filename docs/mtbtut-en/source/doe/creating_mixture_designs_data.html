Data requirements for  

What kind of data is required?

No data is required to use Create Mixture Design. Simply define a design and enter information about your complonents, and Minitab will assemble the data necessary for use in Analyze Mixture Design (minus the response data, which you must collect).

Type of design

The number of mixture components in your experiment, constraints on the formulation and other considerations will dictate the type of design that is appropriate for your circumstances. Minitab offers simplex centroid, simplex lattice, and extreme vertices designs, each of which may be customized to meet the needs of your experiment.

Component information

You must have at least two mixture components. The component bounds must be numeric. For mixture-process variable designs, you can specify up to 7 process variables.

Additional considerations

Various limitations and requirements placed on your experiment can influence the design. Linear constraints placed on mixture components, center points and axial points, and replicates can affect the number and makeup of the experimental runs in your design.

Response data

After you conduct the experiment, enter the response data for each run into the worksheet. You can have up to 25 numeric response data columns. Examples of response variables are:
  • Taste score
  • Yield of a crop
  • Adhesive properties of paint

What should my worksheet look like?

When you create a design, Minitab stores the standard order, run order, point type, block assignment, and each component in separate columns. Each row in the worksheet contains data corresponding to one run of your experiment. The exact makeup of your worksheet will vary depending on your design choices, component information, process variables, and other considerations.
