Description of how to a using Minitab  

Scenario

Researchers in a food laboratory are trying to improve a recipe for cheese fondue. They would like to study how the mixture blend and the serving temperature of the fondue affect the flavor.

The mixture is comprised of three ingredients: two types of Swiss cheese (Emmenthaler and Gruyere) and white wine.

Enter response data in worksheet

After you create the design, conduct the experiment, collect the data, and enter the data in the Minitab worksheet. A worksheet is already prepared for you to analyze.
  1. Open Fondue.MTW.

Specify your model

You can add and remove individual terms from your model or specify the order of the model. Specify a quadratic model that includes all components (the three ingredients) and one process variable (serving temperature).
  1. Choose Stat > DOE > Mixture > Analyze Mixture Design.
  2. In Responses, enter Flavor.
  3. Under Type of Model, choose Mixture components and process variables.
  4. Click Terms.
  5. From Include component terms for model, choose Quadratic.
  6. Click OK.

Add graphs to your output

When you have a final model, create residual plots to check assumptions of normality, constant variance, and randomness. Residual plots also help you to see if another variable affects the response or if outliers are present.
  1. Click Graphs.
  2. Under Residual Plots, choose Four in one.
  3. Check Residuals versus variables and enter Food.
  4. Click OK in each dialog box.

Interpreting the output

Now what? For guidance on interpreting the results of this analysis, see StatGuide. To see a related tutorial that creates this design, see Create Mixture Designs. For a tutorial on graphs associated with this type of design, see Graphs for Mixtures DOE.
