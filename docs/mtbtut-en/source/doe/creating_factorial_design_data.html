Data requirements for  

What kind of data is required?

No data is required to use Create Factorial Design. Simply define a design and enter information about your factors, and Minitab will assemble the data necessary for use in Analyze Factorial Design (minus the response data, which you must collect).

Type of design

The number of factors in your experiment, the number of levels in each factor, and other considerations will dictate the type of design that is appropriate for your circumstances. Minitab offers two-level, Plackett-Burman, and general full factorial designs, each of which may be customized to meet the needs of your experiment.

Factor information

You must have at least two factors and two levels (or more with a general full factorial design) for each factor. Factors can be a categorical or continuous. Factor settings can be text (such as high and low) or numeric (such as 100° and 200°).

Additional considerations

The goals of your experiment may dictate the need for greater or less statistical power (for example, a very sensitive final assessment versus an early screening analysis). Budget restrictions may also limit the total number of experimental runs you can afford to perform. Specifying different numbers of center points and replicates gives you some control over these factors. Minitab provides power and sample size assessments for 2-level factorial, Plackett-Burman, and general full factorial designs to help you choose the design appropriate for your situation.

Response data

After you conduct the experiment, enter the response data for each run into the worksheet. You can have up to 25 numeric response data columns. Examples of response variables are:
  • Yield of a chemical reaction
  • Time to process an order
  • Strength of plastic

What should my worksheet look like?

When you create a factorial design, Minitab stores the standard order, run order, center point indicator, block assignment, and each factor in separate columns. Each row in the worksheet contains data that corresponds to one run of your experiment. The exact makeup of your worksheet will vary depending on your design choices and factor information.
