Description of how to create a mixture design plot using Minitab  

Scenario

Researchers in a food laboratory want to improve a recipe for cheese fondue. They would like to study how the mixture blend and the serving temperature of the fondue affects the flavor.

The mixture is comprised of three ingredients: two types of Swiss cheese (Emmenthaler and Gruyere) and white wine.

Note To illustrate the factorial plots (main effects, interaction, and cube), the serving time (ServeTime) was included in the design. This process variable was not included in the analysis for any other plots.

Create a simplex design plot

Create a simplex design plot to visualize your design at both low and high temperature settings.
  1. Open Fondue.MTW.
  2. Choose Stat > DOE > Mixture > Simplex Design Plot.
  3. Click Settings.
  4. Under Hold process variables at, choose Low setting.
  5. Click OK in each dialog box.
  6. Click [Ctrl ]+ [E].
  7. Click Settings.
  8. Under Hold process variables at, choose High setting.
  9. Click OK in each dialog box.

Create factorial plots

Create factorial plots to see how the process variables (the serving temperature and the time the fondue remained in its serving pot) affect the response.
  1. Open Cheese.MTW.
  2. Choose Stat > DOE > Mixture > Factorial Plots.
  3. Check Main Effects Plot, then click Setup.
  4. In Responses, enter Flavor.
  5. Move Temperature and ServeTime from Available to Selected.
  6. Click OK.
  7. Repeat steps 36 once, checking Interaction plot, and again checking Cube plot.
  8. Click OK.

Create a response trace plot

Create a response trace plot to see how changes in component proportions affect the response relative to a reference blend. Hold the process variables at the high settings.
  1. Open Fondue2.MTW.
  2. Choose Stat > DOE > Mixture > Response Trace Plot.
  3. Click Settings.
  4. Under Hold process variables at, choose High settings.
  5. Click OK in each dialog box.

Create contour and surface plots

Create contour and surface plots to find the component settings that provide the response you want.
  1. Open Fondue2.MTW.
  2. Choose Stat > DOE > Mixture > Contour/Surface Plots.
  3. Check Contour plot, then click Setup.
  4. Under Component Unit in Plot(s), choose Proportion.
  5. Check Plot all level combinations.
  6. Click Contours.
  7. Under Contour Levels, choose Number and type 6.
  8. Click OK twice.
  9. Check Surface Plot, then click Setup.
  10. Under Component Unit in Plot(s), choose Proportion
  11. Click Settings.
  12. Under Hold process variables at, choose High setting.
  13. Click OK in each dialog box.
