Description of how to a using Minitab  

Scenario

A building materials manufacturer is developing a new insulation product. They would like to study four factors that they believe to impact the strength of the insulation. The four factors are:
  • Material type (Material)
  • Injection temperature (InjTemp)
  • Injection pressure (InjPress)
  • Cooling temperature (CoolTemp)

The manufacturer believes that temperature may also impact strength, but, because temperature cannot be controlled during the experiment, it is recorded as a covariate (MeasTemp).

Choose the appropriate design and number of factors

The senior researcher at the materials company wants to conduct a 2-level full factorial design to determine which factors significantly impact the strength of insulation material.
  1. Choose Stat > DOE > Factorial > Create Factorial Design.
  2. Under Type of Design, verify that 2-level factorial (default generators) is selected.
  3. In Number of factors, choose 4.
  4. Click Designs.
  5. In the box at the top, choose Full factorial.
  6. Click OK.

Name factors and define factor settings

You can name each factor, specify the data type (text or numeric), and define the factor settings.
  1. Click Factors and complete the table as follows:
    Factor Name Type Low High
    A Material Text Formula1 Formula2
    B InjPress Numeric 75 150
    C InjTemp Numeric 85 100
    D CoolTemp Numeric 25 45
  2. Click OK in each dialog box.
