Description of how to create a factorial plot using Minitab  

Scenario

A building materials manufacturer is developing a new insulation product. They want to study four factors they believe to impact the strength of the insulation. The four factors are:
  • Material type (Material)
  • Injection temperature (InjTemp)
  • Injection pressure (InjPress)
  • Cooling temperature (CoolTemp)

The manufacturer believes that temperature may also impact strength, but, because termperature cannot be controlled during the experiment, it is recorded as a covariate (MeasTemp).

Specify your model

Include main effects (each of the four factors individually) and all 2-way interactions (all combinations of two factors)in the model. Also include the covariate, MeasTemp, in your model.
  1. Open Insulation.MTW.
  2. Choose Stat > DOE > Factorial > Analyze Factorial Design.
  3. In Responses, enter Strength.
  4. Click Terms.
  5. From Include terms in the model up through order, choose 2.
  6. Click OK.
  7. Click Covariates.
  8. In Covariates, enter MeasTemp.
  9. Click OK in each dialog box.

Create factorial plots and a cube plot

Plot the fitted means on the main effects and interaction plots. Use the data means for the cube plot.
  1. Choose Stat > DOE > Factorial > Factorial Plots.
  2. In Responses, choose Strength.
  3. Move Material, InjPress, InjTemp, and CoolTemp from Available to Selected.
  4. Click Graphs.
  5. Check Main effects plot and Interaction plot.
  6. Click OK in each dialog box.
  7. Choose Stat > DOE > Factorial > Cube Plot.
  8. Under Type of Means to Use in Plots, choose Fitted Means.
  9. In Responses, choose Strength.
  10. Move Material, InjPress, InjTemp, and CoolTemp from Available to Selected.
  11. Click OK.

Create contour and surface plots

Create contour plots and surface plots for InjPress and InjTemp, InjPress and CoolTemp, and InjTemp and CoolTemp to identify their ideal settings.
  1. Choose Stat > DOE > Factorial > Contour Plot.
  2. In Responses, choose Strength.
  3. In Variables, choose Generate plots for all pairs of continuous variables.
  4. Click Contours.
  5. Under Contour Levels, choose Number, then enter 7.
  6. Click OK.
  7. Click Settings.
  8. Under Setting, enter Formula2 for Material, 150 for InjPress, 100 for InjTemp, and 25 for CoolTemp.
  9. Click OK in each dialog box.
  10. Choose Stat > DOE > Factorial > Surface Plot.
  11. Check Surface plot.
  12. In Responses, choose Strength.
  13. In Variables, choose Generate plots for all pairs of continuous variables.
  14. Click Settings.
  15. Under Setting, enter Formula2 for Material, 150 for InjPress, 100 for InjTemp, and 25 for CoolTemp.
  16. Click OK in each dialog box.
