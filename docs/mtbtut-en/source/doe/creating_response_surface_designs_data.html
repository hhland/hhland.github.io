Data requirements for  

What kind of data is required?

No data is required to use Create Response Surface Design. Simply define a design and enter information about your factors, and Minitab will assemble the data necessary for use in Analyze Response Surface Design (minus the response data, which you must collect).

Type of design

Your goals for the experiment, budgetary or practical limitations on the runs that can be performed, and other considerations will dictate the type of design that is appropriate for your circumstances. Minitab offers central composite and Box-Behnken designs, both of which may be customized to meet the needs of your experiment.

Factor information

You can have two to ten factors. Factors can be categorical or continuous. Factor settings can be text (such as high and low) or numeric (such as 100° and 200°).

Additional considerations

Various limitations and requirements placed on your experiment can influence the design. You can specify center points and replicates to affect the number and makeup of the experimental runs in your design.

Response data

After you conduct the experiment, enter the response data for each run into the worksheet. You can have up to 25 numeric response data columns. Examples of response variables are:
  • Yield of a chemical reaction
  • Time to process an order
  • Strength of plastic

What should my worksheet look like?

When you create a design, Minitab stores the standard order, run order, point type, block assignment, and each factor in separate columns. Each row in the worksheet contains data that corresponds to one run of your experiment.

If you specify factor level settings, Minitab will display the actual values in the worksheet. Otherwise, Minitab displays the factor levels in coded units: -1 for the low value, 0 for the center point, and 1 for the high value. Axial points are coded as -1.41421 and 1.41421. In either case, you usually want to analyze your design in coded units.
