Description of how to a using Minitab  

Scenario

A building materials manufacturer is developing a new insulation product. They would like to study four factors they believe to impact the strength of the insulation. The four factors are:
  • Material type (Material)
  • Injection temperature (InjTemp)
  • Injection pressure (InjPress)
  • Cooling temperature (CoolTemp)

The manufacturer believes that temperature may also impact strength, but, because it cannot be controlled during the experiment, it is recorded as a covariate (MeasTemp).

Enter response data in worksheet

After you create the design, conduct the experiment, collect the response data, and enter the data in the Minitab worksheet. A worksheet is already prepared for you to analyze.
  1. Open Insulation.MTW.

Specify your model

You can add and remove individual terms from your model or specify the order of the model. For this example, include main effects (each of the four factors, individually) and all 2-way interactions (all combinations of two factors) in the model. Also include the covariate, MeasTemp, in your model.
  1. Choose Stat > DOE > Factorial > Analyze Factorial Design.
  2. In Responses, enter Strength.
  3. Click Terms.
  4. From Include terms in the model up through order, choose 2.
  5. Click OK.
  6. Click Covariates.
  7. In Covariates, enter MeasTemp.
  8. Click OK.

Add graphs to your output

Create effects plots to identify influential factorsÂ—in this case, the factors that influence insulation strength. When you have a final model, create residual plots to check assumptions of normality, constant variance, and randomness. Residuals plots also help you to see if another variable affects the response or if outliers are present.
  1. Click Graphs.
  2. Under Effects Plots, check Pareto, Normal, and Half Normal.
  3. Under Residuals Plots, choose Four in one.
  4. Check Residuals versus variables and enter Machine.
  5. Click OK in each dialog box.

Interpreting the output

Now what? For guidance on interpreting the results of this analysis, see StatGuide. To see a related tutorial that creates this design, see Create Factorial Designs. For a tutorial on graphs associated with this type of design, see Graphs for Factorial DOE.
