Data requirements for 1-Sample t  

What kind of data is required?

Your measurement data must be numeric and continuous. For example:
  • Weight of bags of coffee beans
  • Distance from employees' homes to their workplaces
  • Time to assemble a car

The data should also follow a normal distribution, although the t-test is robust against violations of this assumption for large sample sizes (approximately greater than 30). The data should also be independent and randomly collected.

Consider how much data you need to ensure that the test has enough power to detect a difference between the population mean and a target value. To help determine the sample size that you need, use Stat > Power and Sample Size > 1-Sample t.

What should my worksheet look like?

Raw data

Raw data lists every observation. To enter raw data, enter a single column of numeric data. If you enter multiple columns, Minitab performs a 1-Sample t analysis for each column.

Summarized data

To enter summarized data, you do not need data in a worksheet. Simply type the mean, sample size, and standard deviation of your sample into the dialog box.
