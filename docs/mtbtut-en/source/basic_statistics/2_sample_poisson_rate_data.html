Data requirements for 2-Sample Poisson Rate  

What kind of data is required?

Your data must follow a Poisson distribution. In other words, they must represent counts of events, defects, or activities. Also, the data must be nonnegative integers. For example:
  • The number of pedestrians that use two different crosswalk each day
  • The number of flaws on a car's new coat of paint produced on two finishing lines each shift
  • The number of 911 calls per hour in two calling areas

Consider how much data you need to ensure the test has enough power to detect differences between the two groups. To help determine the sample size you need, use Stat > Power and Sample Size > 2-Sample Poisson Rate.

What should my worksheet look like?

Raw data

Raw data can be stacked or unstacked.

To enter stacked raw data, list observations from both samples in one column, and provide a second column of subscripts to identify the sample.
C1 C2
Defects Subscripts
1 1 Night
2 3 Night
3 2 Night
4 0 Night
5 1 Night
6 5 Day
7 4 Day
8 6 Day
9 5 Day

To enter unstacked raw data, list every observation from each sample in a single column.
C1 C2
Night Day
1 1 5
2 3 4
3 2 6
4 0 5
5 1  

Frequency data

Frequency data can be stacked or unstacked.

To enter stacked frequency data, list the unique values from both samples in a single column. List subscripts to identify the sample of each observation in a second column. Finally, list the frequencies for each unique value in a third column.
C1 C2 C3
Defects Subscripts Frequency
1 0 Night 2
2 1 Night 2
3 2 Night 1
4 1 Day 1
5 4 Day 3
6 5 Day 1

To enter unstacked frequency data, list the unique values from each sample in a separate column, and list their frequencies in two additional columns.
C1 C2 C3 C4
Night Frequency_Night Day Frequency_Day
1 0 2 1 1
2 1 2 4 3
3 2 1 5 1

Summarized data

To enter summarized data, you do not need data in a worksheet. Simply type the number of occurrences and the sample size for each sample into the dialog box. For Poisson data, sample size equals the number of units over which items are counted. For example, if you counted the number of defects on two production lines over 30 days, the sample size is 30.
