Description of how to perform a 2-sample t-test using Minitab  

Scenario

A health management firm has samples of satisfaction ratings from former patients of two hospitals. The firm wants to know if one hospital is rated more highly than the other. The firm will use the information to refer patients and to make suggestions for hospital improvement. The variances of the two samples are sufficiently similar, so the firm will use a pooled standard deviation for the test.

Choose the appropriate analysis

Because you want to compare the means of two independent groups, a 2-sample t-test is appropriate.
  1. Open the worksheet HOSPITALRATINGS.MTW.
  2. Choose Stat > Basic Statistics > 2-Sample t.

Choose the appropriate data setup option and enter your data.

These observations have been organized as both stacked (Rate and Hospital) data and unstacked (A and B) data, so you can enter your data using either the Both samples are in one column option or the Each sample is in its own column option. In this example, we'll use the unstacked data.
  1. Choose Each sample is in its own column.
  2. In Sample 1, enter A.
  3. In Sample 2, enter B.
  4. Click Options.
  5. Check Assume equal variances.
  6. Click OK.

Specify a test difference, if necessary

For this 2-sample t-test, you want to know if the ratings differ, but, if you wanted to know if they differed by a particular amount or if a particular group had higher or lower ratings, you would specify that in Options.
  • (no action required)

Add graphs to your output

Both boxplots and individual value plots can help you understand how your groups differ.
  1. Click Graphs, and then check Individual value plot and Boxplot.
  2. Click OK in each dialog box.
