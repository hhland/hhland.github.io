Description of how to perform 2 Variances using Minitab  

Scenario

A recent study compared drivers on two types of roads. Each driver drove on one of two road types: paved (1) and unpaved dirt (2). To measure driving performance, testers recorded the number of steering corrections that each driver made on each type of road. The testers want to determine whether the drivers' performances were equally variable across the two road conditions.

Choose the appropriate analysis

Testers want to compare the variances of two groups. 2 Variances is appropriate for this situation.
  1. Open the worksheet ROAD.MTW.
  2. Choose Stat > Basic Statistics > 2 Variances.

Specify data source

Specify whether you have raw data (organized as a single column of sample observations with a column of subscripts, or as two columns of sample observations) or summary data (stated in terms of standard deviations or variances).
  1. Choose Both sample are in one column.

Enter your data

This worksheet contains stacked data in a single column, with a second column of sample IDs.
  1. In Samples, enter Corrects.
  2. In Sample IDs, enter RoadType.
  3. Click OK.

Specify options

This 2 variance analysis requires no further modifications, but other options are available. Use the Options subdialog box for example to change the confidence level, to test a particular ratio value, or to specify a one-sided test. Use the Graphs subdialog box to request a summary plot, histogram, or individual value plot.
  • (no action required)
