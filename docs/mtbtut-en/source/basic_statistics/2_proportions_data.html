Data requirements for 2 Proportions  

What kind of data is required?

For each sample, the data must record the outcomes of a series of independent trials that have only two possible outcomes. For example:
  • In a crate of 24 soda battles, 2 lost their carbonation.
  • Of 300,000 eligible voters, 190,000 voted in an election.
  • Of 30 patients, 22 benefitted from an experimental drug.

Consider how much data you need to ensure that the test has enough power to detect differences between groups. To help determine the sample size that you need, use Stat > Power and Sample Size > 2 Proportions.

What should my worksheet look like?

Raw data

Raw data explicitly list the outcome of every trial. Data can be text or numeric, with a maximum of two distinct values: one to represent the event of interest, and one to represent a "nonevent".

Minitab uses your data to calculate a proportion of events. Minitab determines events and nonevents by value order (numeric, alphabetic, or an order that you define). Minitab defines observations that are greater (greater numerical value, or the text value closer to the end of the alphabet) as the event. For example, in series of "Heads or Tails" or "0 or 1" trials, "Tails" and "1" are the events; "Heads" and "0" are the nonevents.

Raw data can be stacked or unstacked.

To enter stacked raw data, enter the outcomes of trials for both samples. Provide a second column of subscripts to identify the sample.

Summarized data

To enter summarized data, you do not need data in a worksheet. Simply type the number of trials and the number of events in each sample into the dialog box.
