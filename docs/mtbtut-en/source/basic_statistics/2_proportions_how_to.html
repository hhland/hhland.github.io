Description of how to perform 2 Proportions using Minitab  

Scenario

A university financial-aid office polled its undergraduate students to determine if male or female students were more likely to obtain summer employment. Of the 802 male students sampled, 725 were employed during the summer, and, of the 712 female students sampled, 573 were employed.

Choose the appropriate analysis

This example compares the proportion of events in two groups, so 2 Proportions is appropriate.

  1. Choose Stat > Basic Statistics > 2 Proportions.

Enter your data

The office records the numbers of trials (number of students sampled) and events (number of students with summer employment) in both groups, so they enter summarized data.
  1. Choose Summarized data.
  2. For Sample 1, in Number of events type 725, and in Number of trials type 802.
  3. For the Sample 2, in Number of events type 573, and in Number of trials type 712.
  4. Click OK.

Specify options

This 2 proportions analysis requires no further modifications, but other options are available. Use the Options subdialog box to change the confidence level or test difference, to specify a one-sided hypothesis test, or to specify a pooled estimate of the true proportion for the normal approximation test.
  • (no action required)
