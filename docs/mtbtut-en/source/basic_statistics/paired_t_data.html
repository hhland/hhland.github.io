Data requirements for Paired t  

What kind of data is required?

Your data must be numeric measurements of two dependent samples. (If your samples are independent, compare them with 2-Sample t instead.) For example:
  • Skill assessment scores of employees before and after they attend a training program
  • Participants' weight before and after a 3 month diet plan
  • Yields of two varieties of wheat, after 20 farmers agree to plant half their fields with each variety

Consider how much data you need to ensure that the test has enough power to detect differences between dependent groups. To help determine the sample size that you need, use Stat > Power and Sample Size > Paired t.

What should my worksheet look like?

Raw data

To enter raw data, list observations from each sample in a separate column. Each row corresponds to a pair of dependent observations. The columns must have the same length.

Summarized data

To enter summarized data, you do not need data in a worksheet. Simply type the sample size, mean difference, and standard deviation of the difference into the dialog box.
