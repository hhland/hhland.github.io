Data requirements for 1 Proportion  

What kind of data is required?

The data must record the outcomes of a series of independent trials that have only two possible outcomes. For example:
  • 100 cell phone batteries underwent an initial burn-in test, which 10 failed.
  • The stitching on 500 shoes was inspected by a quality control department. 45 shoes were defective and required rework.
  • Of 12 pretzel bags, 2 had defective seals.

Consider how much data you need to ensure that the test has enough power to detect differences between your population proportion and a target value. To help determine the sample size that you need, use Stat > Power and Sample Size > 1 Proportion.

What should my worksheet look like?

Raw data

Enter a single column of text or numeric values, with two distinct values: one to represent the "event" of interest (a success) and one to represent a "nonevent" (a failure).

Minitab uses your data to calculate a proportion of events. Minitab determines events and nonevents by value order (numeric, alphabetic, or an order that you define). Minitab defines observations that are greater (greater numerical value, or the text value closer to the end of the alphabet) as the event. For example, in series of "Heads or Tails" or "0 or 1" trials, "Tails" and "1" are the events; "Heads" and "0" are the nonevents.

If you enter multiple columns, Minitab performs a 1 Proportion analysis for each column.

Summarized data

To enter summarized data, you do not need data in a worksheet. Simply type the number of trials and the number of events into the dialog box.
