Data requirements for General Linear Model ANOVA  

What kind of data is required?

You must have a single, continuous response variable and one or more categorical or continuous predictor variables. Categorical predictors are called "factors" and continuous predictors are called "covariates". For example:
  • A steel strength score with the plant location, shift, and the molten steel temperature values
  • A braking distance measurement with the brake pad type, automobile model, and tire model
  • A daily ice cream sales total with the day, month, and high temperature

GLM's versatility in performing ANOVA analyses extends to its data requirements. Factors can be crossed or nested, fixed or random. Covariates can be crossed with each other or with factors, or nested within factors.

What should my worksheet look like?

Set up your worksheet with one column for each response variable, factor, and covariate. Each row in your worksheet represents measurements on one observation or subject. If a row contains missing data, Minitab excludes the entire row from all computations.
