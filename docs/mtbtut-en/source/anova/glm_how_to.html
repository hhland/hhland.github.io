Description of how to perform a General Linear Model ANOVA using Minitab  

Scenario

A sociologist randomly sampled 45 professors who taught an evening course at a large, metropolitan university. The data collected includes:
  • Subject: The subject matter of the course: humanities (1), social sciences (2), engineering (3), or management (4)
  • Degree: The highest degree that the professor possesses: Bachelor's (1), Master's (2), or Ph.D. (3)
  • Salary: The amount of payment received for teaching the course (in thousands of dollars).

The sociologist wants to examine the effects of degree and subject on salary.

Choose the appropriate analysis

The data are unbalanced and there are two factors, so the sociologist uses the GLM procedure to analyze them.
  1. Open the worksheet PROFESSOR.MTW.
  2. Choose Stat > ANOVA > General Linear Model > Fit General Linear Model.

Enter your response variable

Enter a column of continuous measurements for the response variable.
  1. In Responses, enter Salary.

Enter the factors and covariates in your model

Each factor and covariate represents a column of data for each variable.

You must enter all of the terms in your model here, even if you need to further specify your model.
  1. In Factors, enter Subject Degree.

Further specify your model

The flexibility of GLM allows you to specify different types of terms. For instance, you may include interaction, higher-order, and nested terms. To specify these terms, you must perform additional actions in different dialog boxes. These terms do not correspond to a single column of data. In this example, the sociologist includes the interaction term.

If you have covariates, enter them in Covariates in the main dialog. If your model includes random factors or nested factors, click Random/Nest and specify them there. This model includes neither.

To specify interaction terms and polynomial terms, click Model.
  1. Click Model.
  2. Under Factors and covariates, press the Ctrl key and select both Subject Degree.
  3. To the right of Interactions through order, choose 2, and click Add.
  4. Click OK.

Add graphs to your output

Viewing the residual plots to check GLM assumptions is important. The plots indicate whether you can trust the test results. The Main Effects Plot and Interactions Plot help you to visualize the relationship between the predictors and the response.
  1. Click Graphs, then choose Four in one.
  2. In Residuals versus the variables, enter Semester.
  3. Click OK in each dialog box.

Specify options

This analysis requires no further modifications, but other options are available. To change the confidence level, transform nonnormal data with the Box-Cox transformation, and other aspects of the analysis, click Options. To control how Minitab codes categorical predictors and to standardize continuous predictors, click Coding. If you want to use a stepwise procedure to choose your model, click Stepwise. To modify the display of analysis results, click Results. To store GLM statistics, click Storage.
  • (no action required)

Compare means after fitting the model

After you fit the model, you can use the stored to model to easily perform additional analyses. For this example, we' will perform two additional analyses.

Use Comparisons to estimate the differences between specific groups, select a multiple comparison method. Each method uses a different approach. Choosing the proper method is important because the method can change the results. Generally, you choose just one; however, for the sake of illustration, select several.
  1. Choose Stat > ANOVA > General Linear Model > Comparisons.
  2. Under Method, check Tukey, Bonferroni, and Sidak.
  3. In Choose terms for comparisons, double-click Subject.
  4. Click OK.

Generate factorial plots after fitting the model

Use Factorial Plots to display the Main Effects Plot and Interactions Plot to help you visualize the relationship between the predictors and the response.
  1. Choose Stat > ANOVA > General Linear Model > Factorial Plots.
  2. The default settings are correct, so you click OK.
